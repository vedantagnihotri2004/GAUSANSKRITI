Indigenous cow breed heritage

Gir

Heat-resistant and high-yielding, Gir cows from Gujarat are known for their rich, nutritious milk

Sahiwal

Punjab's finest milking breed, Sahiwal cows excel in heat tolerance, disease resistance, and superior milk quality.

Red Sindhi

Hardy and adaptable, Red Sindhi cows from Sindh thrive in hot climates, producing high-fat milk.

Tharparkar

Originating from Rajasthan's Thar Desert, Tharparkar cows are drought-resistant, dual-purpose, and high milk yielders.

Kankrej

Gujarat-Rajasthan's Kankrej breed is strong, disease-resistant, and valued for both milk production and draught power.

Ongole

Andhra Pradesh's Ongole breed is globally renowned for its strength, disease resistance, and dairy potential.

Hallikar

Karnataka's Hallikar cattle are powerful draught animals, widely used for plowing and traditional agricultural activities.

Vechur

Kerala's Vechur cow, the world's smallest breed, produces highly nutritious milk with medicinal properties.

Key Problems In Indigenous Cattle Sector

Population Decline

Rapid decrease in pure indigenous cattle population due to crossbreeding and lack of preservation efforts.

Knowledge Gap

Loss of traditional knowledge and lack of awareness about indigenous breeds' benefits.

Economic Viability

Lower milk yields making indigenous cattle farming less profitable for farmers.

Genetic Degradation

Poor breeding practices leading to loss of pure indigenous genetic traits.

Health Monitoring

Limited access to veterinary care and lack of real-time health monitoring systems.

Product Utilization

Underutilization of valuable cow-based products and lack of proper market channels.

Data Management

Inefficient farm data tracking and lack of analytical insights for decision making.

Government Support

Limited integration with government schemes and NGO support programs.
